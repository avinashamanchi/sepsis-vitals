## 3. Risk Management (ISO 14971)

### 3.1 Risk Register

| ID | Hazard | Severity | Probability | Risk | Mitigation | Residual |
|----|--------|----------|-------------|------|------------|----------|
| R-001 | False negative (missed sepsis) | Catastrophic | Possible | High | Multi-score ensemble, mandatory vital re-check at 4h | Medium |
| R-002 | False positive (alert fatigue) | Serious | Probable | High | Threshold tuning, fatigue monitoring, auto-suppression | Medium |
| R-003 | System downtime | Serious | Unlikely | Medium | Multi-AZ RDS, ECS auto-rollback, offline scoring mode | Low |
| R-004 | Data breach (PII exposure) | Serious | Unlikely | Medium | AES-256 encryption, RBAC, MFA, WAF, audit logging | Low |
| R-005 | Model drift (degraded accuracy) | Moderate | Possible | Medium | PSI monitoring, monthly recalibration checks | Low |
| R-006 | Prompt injection (AI misuse) | Moderate | Unlikely | Low | 12-pattern regex, structural isolation, rate limiting | Low |
| R-007 | Biased predictions (subgroup harm) | Serious | Possible | High | Fairness audit, conformal prediction, subgroup monitoring | Medium |
| R-008 | Webhook tampering | Moderate | Unlikely | Low | HMAC-SHA256, 5-min replay window | Low |
| R-009 | Unauthorized access | Serious | Unlikely | Medium | JWT RS256, MFA, exponential lockout, CORS whitelist | Low |

## 5. Change Control

All changes to clinical scoring algorithms require:
1. Literature review justification
2. Impact analysis on existing risk assessments
3. Updated test cases covering new behavior
4. Code review by at least 1 clinical domain expert
5. Regression testing (full 187-test suite)
6. Updated risk register if new hazards identified
7. Version bump and changelog entry
