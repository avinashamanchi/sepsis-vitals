The Problem
Sepsis is the #1 killer in hospitals
Every hour of delayed treatment increases mortality by 7.6%. In low-resource hospitals, sepsis is often caught too late.
11M

Deaths per year

Sepsis kills more people than cancer. 85% of cases occur in low- and middle-income countries where diagnostic tools are scarce.

7.6%

Hourly mortality increase

Each hour of delayed antibiotic administration increases the risk of death. Early detection is the single most impactful intervention.

80%

Lack early warning

Most district hospitals in LMICs have no automated early warning system. Nurses manually track vitals on paper with no algorithmic support.

Security
Six layers of protection
Medical-grade security from day one. Every layer tested, every secret managed.

Rate Limiting

Token-bucket algorithm (5 req/s API, 0.5 req/s LLM) + AWS WAF rules

Secret Management

AWS Secrets Manager, env-only config, gitleaks in CI. Zero hard-coded keys.

Endpoint Protection

CORS whitelist, WAF, CSP headers, no wildcard origins

Encryption

AES-256-GCM for PII, Fernet for config, RDS KMS, TLS 1.3 everywhere

Prompt Injection Guard

12-pattern regex + structural isolation + client-side pre-filter

Webhook Security

HMAC-SHA256 signatures with 5-minute replay window
